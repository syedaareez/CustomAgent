## Business Requirements

| ID | Requirement | Priority | Rationale |
|----|-------------|----------|-----------|
| BR-001 | The About Me page must present a concise professional summary with role/title, value proposition, top skills, and a prominent recruiter contact CTA. Acceptance criteria: Given a recruiter visits the page, when they view the hero section, then they can identify the candidate's title, value proposition, 3 key skills, and a visible contact link within 5 seconds. | Must Have | Recruiters need a clear first impression and an easy way to initiate contact. |
| BR-002 | The page must use a recruiter-focused structure with distinct sections for headline, professional summary, experience highlights, strengths, personal story, and contact details. Acceptance criteria: Given the page is loaded, when a recruiter scrolls, then they can easily locate each section and understand the candidate's background without confusion. | Must Have | A clear structure improves scanability and recruiter comprehension. |
| BR-003 | The About Me page must be mobile-first, responsive, and accessible with readable typography, sufficient contrast, and keyboard navigability. Acceptance criteria: Given the page is viewed on common mobile and desktop screen sizes, when evaluated with accessibility tools, then it passes basic responsive display checks and WCAG AA contrast guidelines. | Must Have | Recruiters access the page on varied devices and need an accessible experience. |
| BR-004 | The page should include technical and UX optimizations such as SEO-friendly metadata, fast load performance, and a recruiter-focused contact/resume action. Acceptance criteria: Given a page load, when measured, then it should load within acceptable performance thresholds and display metadata for search engines; when a recruiter clicks the contact action, then they can initiate email or resume download. | Should Have | Performance and discoverability increase recruiter reach and engagement. |
| BR-005 | The page could feature trust signals such as career achievements, selected client or employer logos, and optional testimonials to reinforce candidate credibility. Acceptance criteria: Given the page is reviewed, when the recruiter inspects credibility content, then they can identify at least two achievement or trust signal elements supporting the candidate's experience. | Could Have | Trust signals help recruiters assess reliability and fit more quickly. |
